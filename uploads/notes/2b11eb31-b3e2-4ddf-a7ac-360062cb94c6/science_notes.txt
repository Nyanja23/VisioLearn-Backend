1. WHAT ARE LIVING THINGS?
---------------------------
Living things are things that are ALIVE.
In Uganda, we see many living things around us every day.

Examples of Living Things in Uganda:
- Animals: Cows, goats, chickens, dogs, cats, lions (in parks)
- People: You, your teacher, your parents, your baby brother
- Plants: Mango trees, banana plants (matoke), maize, beans, grass
- Birds: Weaverbirds, parrots, hens
- Insects: Grasshoppers (nsenene), ants, butterflies

2. WHAT ARE NON-LIVING THINGS?
-------------------------------
Non-living things are things that are NOT ALIVE.
They do not eat, grow, move by themselves, or feel.

Examples of Non-Living Things in Uganda:
- Stones, sand, soil (when dead/dry)
- Bricks used for building houses
- School items: Pencil, book, ruler, desk, chalkboard
- Home items: Pot, pan, plate, cup, sofa, bed
- Water in a bottle, a bicycle, a car, a hoe

3. DIFFERENCES BETWEEN LIVING AND NON-LIVING THINGS
----------------------------------------------------
Let us use a cow (living) and a stone (non-living) to compare.

| FEATURE                | LIVING THINGS (e.g., Cow)      | NON-LIVING THINGS (e.g., Stone) |
|------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Movement               | Can move by itself             | Cannot move by itself            |
| Growth                 | Grows bigger and older         | Does not grow                    |
| Breathing/Respiration  | Breathes air (nose/lungs)      | Does not breathe                 |
| Feeding/Nutrition      | Needs food and water           | Does not eat or drink            |
| Reproduction           | Produces young ones (calf)     | Does not produce young ones      |
| Feeling/Sensitivity    | Feels pain, heat, cold         | Does not feel                    |
| Wasting/Excretion      | Produces waste (droppings, urine)| Does not produce waste          |
| Death                  | Dies one day                   | Does not die (just breaks)       |

4. CHARACTERISTICS OF LIVING THINGS (MRS GREN)
-----------------------------------------------
To remember easily, use MRS GREN:

M - Movement (move their own body parts)
R - Respiration (breathe air)
S - Sensitivity (feel changes around them)
G - Growth (get taller, bigger, heavier)
R - Reproduction (give birth to or produce young ones)
E - Excretion (remove waste from body)
N - Nutrition (eat and drink)

All living things do ALL these things. Non-living things do NONE.

7. FUN FACT!
------------
Even a seed (like a bean seed) is a living thing. It is sleeping. When you put it in wet soil, it will wake up and grow into a plant!
